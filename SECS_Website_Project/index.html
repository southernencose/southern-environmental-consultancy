About Us

Our Story

Southern Environmental Consultancy Services (SECS) is a proud 100% nationally owned and managed environmental and social consultancy firm in Papua New Guinea. Established to bridge the gap between development and environmental stewardship, we specialize in delivering scientific research-based solutions that promote sustainable growth while safeguarding PNG's unique natural and cultural heritage.

We are registered with PNG IPA (Reg. #: 6-131329911) and PNG IRC, ensuring legal business compliance. Strategically located in Port Moresby, we maintain a strong network within government, private, and industry sectors, aligning with the Government's Public-Private Partnership (PPP) Arrangement.

Discuss Your Project

Woodlark Island Gold Mine Project

Milne Bay Province, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staffs were recently engaged to conduct the socio-economic Impact Assessment and Sustainability Plan of the impacted communities. This involved a thorough assessment of the benefits and potential impacts of the resettlement package and the relocation program for the displaced communities associated with the Woodlark Island Gold Mine Project. Our work aimed to ensure that community concerns were addressed and that the resettlement process was conducted equitably and sustainably.

The project highlights our expertise in conducting comprehensive socio-economic studies and developing actionable plans that facilitate community well-being alongside large-scale resource development.

Elk Antelope Gas Field Development

Purari River, Gulf Province, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staff were responsible for conducting comprehensive environmental and social impact studies for the Elk Antelope Gas Field Development. This involved extensive biological and physical surveys to establish baseline conditions and assess potential impacts on the surrounding environment and communities. Our efforts were crucial in facilitating the acquisition of the necessary Environmental Permit for the project, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements and promoting sustainable resource development in the region.

This project demonstrates our capability in managing complex environmental assessments for large-scale energy projects, from initial surveys to regulatory approvals.

Edevu Hydro Power Project

Lower Brown River, Central Province, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staff were engaged to conduct a comprehensive assessment of the environmental and social impacts associated with the development of the 30-meter Edevu Hydro Power Project. Our scope of work included detailed environmental and social impact studies, project management support, and crucial community consultations to ensure that the project proceeded with minimal adverse effects on local populations and the ecosystem. This project demonstrates our capability in navigating complex infrastructure developments, ensuring environmental safeguards and community engagement are prioritized.

Curtain Brothers Cargo Wharf Development

Motukea, Port Moresby, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staff undertook the Environmental Impact Statement (EISA) for the development of the commercial cargo wharf at Motukea. This comprehensive assessment covered various aspects, including the impacts of dredging activities, analysis of harbor currents, and detailed ecological assessments of both marine and terrestrial environments. Our meticulous work was instrumental in securing the necessary Environmental Permit for this significant infrastructure project, ensuring that the development adhered to strict environmental standards and sustainable practices.

This project showcases our expertise in handling complex coastal infrastructure projects, integrating environmental protection with commercial development needs.

Abau Oil Palm Project

Abau District, Central Province, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staff conducted the Environmental Impact Statement (EISA) for the development of a 9000-hectare oil palm estate in Abau District. This extensive assessment included adherence to both local environmental regulations and international standards, such as those of the Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO). Our detailed EIA studies ensured a thorough understanding of potential environmental and social impacts, leading to the successful acquisition of the necessary Environmental Permit. This project demonstrates our commitment to sustainable agricultural practices and our ability to manage large-scale developments responsibly.

South Seas Tuna Corporation EIS

Wewak, East Sepik, Papua New Guinea

Project Overview

SECS staff conducted the Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) for the South Seas Tuna Corporation project. This involved a detailed examination of ocean currents and the potential impacts of factory discharges on marine ecosystems. Comprehensive marine and terrestrial surveys were undertaken to assess environmental conditions and identify potential risks. Our thorough assessment and subsequent mitigation strategies were crucial in securing the Environmental Permit for the project, ensuring environmentally responsible operations for this significant fisheries development.

This project highlights our expertise in marine environmental impact assessments and our commitment to sustainable fisheries development.
